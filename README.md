# Zurich Housing & Demographics Visualization Project

This project analyzes and visualizes several aspects of urban development in the city of Zurich, including demographic change, housing expansion, income evolution, and residential construction patterns. Multiple open datasets from *Open Data Zürich* are combined to create a comprehensive set of visualizations.

All figures are automatically generated by running **main.ipynb**.

##  Running the Project

To generate all visualizations:

1. Open **main.ipynb** in Jupyter Notebook, JupyterLab, or VS Code.
2. Run the notebook **from top to bottom**.

All plots will be automatically saved to the \`Plots/\` directory.

##  Visualization Overview

The notebook produces several visualizations, each highlighting different dimensions of Zurich’s population, housing, and income development.


---

### **1. Absolute Growth Analysis**
**File:** \`Bar_abs.png\`  
For the period **2020–2024**, compares the absolute increase in:

- Total apartments  
- Total living area  
- Total population  

Three aligned bar charts provide an intuitive comparison.

---

### **2. Relative Growth Shares + Deviations**
**File:** \`Bar_rel.png\`

Two vertically stacked charts:

1. **Relative shares** (%) of each district in the citywide total  
2. **Difference** in percentage points relative to the apartment share  

Helps identify districts that grew “more in people than in housing” or vice versa.

---

### **3. Choropleth Maps: Apartments by District**
**Files:**  
- \`Map_2008.png\`  
- \`Map_2016.png\`  
- \`Map_2024.png\`

Each map visualizes:

- Number of apartments per district  
- District labels and values  
- Small embedded bar chart  
- Total KPIs for the selected year  

---

### **4. Line Plot: Housing Stock by Construction Period**
**File:** \`Line.png\`  
Shows z-standardized trends in **rooms per apartment** and **living area per apartment** across construction periods for each district.

---

### **5. Waffle Charts: Demographic Composition**
**File:** \`Waffle.png\`

For each district (2020–2024):

- % Children  
- % Retirees  
- % Rest of population  

Represented in a 10×10 waffle grid per district.

---

### **6. Bubble Chart: Income vs Population vs Apartments**
**File:** \`Bubble.png\`

Encodes for each district-year:

- x-axis: median taxable income  
- y-axis: total population  
- bubble size: number of apartments  
- color: district ID  
- transparency: older years vs latest year  

Reveals socioeconomic clustering and development over time.
